School Age

As children enter school, support their academic and social development. Encourage hobbies, extracurricular activities, and independence. Set clear rules and expectations while maintaining open communication.

School Involvement

Ways parents can be involved in their child's education:

  • Attend School Events: Participate in parent-teacher conferences, open houses, and school performances.

  • Communicate with Teachers: Stay in touch with your child's teachers to monitor progress and address any concerns.

  • Volunteer: Offer to volunteer at school events, in the classroom, or with extracurricular activities.

  • Support Learning at Home: Create a supportive learning environment at home and help with homework and projects.

  • Advocate for Your Child: Advocate for your child's needs and communicate with school staff to ensure they receive necessary support.

Homework Help

Strategies for assisting children with homework:

  • Establish a Routine: Set aside a specific time and place for homework each day to create a consistent routine.

  • Provide Support: Offer guidance and assistance with homework tasks, but encourage independence and problem-solving skills.

  • Stay Organized: Help your child stay organized by keeping track of assignments, due dates, and materials needed.

  • Offer Encouragement: Provide praise and encouragement to boost confidence and motivation.

  • Communicate with Teachers: Stay informed about homework assignments and communicate with teachers if your child is struggling.

Extracurricular Activities

Benefits of participating in extracurricular activities:

  • Develops Skills: Extracurricular activities provide opportunities to develop new skills and interests.

  • Builds Confidence: Success in extracurriculars boosts self-esteem and confidence.

  • Encourages Socialization: Participation in group activities fosters friendships and social skills.

  • Enhances Well-Being: Engaging in enjoyable activities outside of school contributes to overall well-being and happiness.

  • Explores Passions: Extracurriculars allow children to explore their passions and hobbies.

Managing Screen Time

Tips for managing screen time for school-age children:

  • Set Limits: Establish clear rules and limits on screen time, including duration and types of content allowed.

  • Encourage Balance: Encourage a balance between screen time and other activities, such as outdoor play, reading, and hobbies.

  • Monitor Content: Monitor the content your child consumes and ensure it is age-appropriate and educational.

  • Model Healthy Habits: Be a positive role model by demonstrating healthy screen habits yourself.

  • Engage in Alternative Activities: Provide alternatives to screen time, such as board games, arts and crafts, and physical activities.
